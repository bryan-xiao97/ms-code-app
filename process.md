# Sell-Side M&A Process — Investment Banker's Playbook

> **Purpose:** Structured knowledge source for an AI agent supporting sell-side M&A deal execution from the investment banker's perspective.
> **Total estimated timeline:** ~4–6 months across 5 phases.

---

## Phase 1: Preparation and Kick Off (~2 weeks)

**Objective:** Align on objectives, stakeholders, and set the foundation for diligence materials.

### 1.1 Create "Sandbox"

- Deal is marked as mandated.
- Draft initial diligence request list.
- Collect deal information (emails, presentations, etc.) and forward to deal distribution list.

**Inputs:** Prior firm materials related to current deal.
**Output → Information Request List:** List of diligence item requests from the bank to the client.

### 1.2 Initiate Due Diligence

- Review company operational and financial performance.

### 1.3 Kickoff Deal

- Prepare kickoff deck outlining discussion topics (timetable, who is involved in the process, etc.).
- Host kickoff meeting with client.
- Set up virtual data room (VDR) and begin collecting deal files.

**Inputs:** Client business materials (market position, products, tailwinds, growth story).
**Output → Kick-Off Organizational Materials:** Covers internal and external workstreams including diligence process structure, VDR setup, third-party reviews, team coordination, legal structuring, and timeline planning.

---

## Phase 2: Marketing Preparation and CIM (~2–3 months)

**Objective:** Conduct bank review of client's business, facilitate third-party diligence, begin building integrated financial model, and create CIM.

### 2.1 Create CIM

- Begin drafting CIM based on meeting notes from client.
- Iterate on CIM; review with banking team.
- Finalize CIM.

**Output → CIM (Confidential Information Memorandum):** Investment memorandum capturing investment highlights, metrics, team capabilities, financial projections, etc.

### 2.2 Conduct Bank Diligence

- Follow-up requests and meetings with client for diligence items.
- Review files and information received from client.
- Update bank diligence tracker based on received client information.
- Incorporate received data into CIM and preliminary VDR.
- Prepare Data Pack / Data Cube with quantitative analysis of the client's business.

**Output → Bank Diligence Tracker:** Tracker to get smart on the client's business, including analysis of suppliers, financial statements, product data, supply chain, etc.

### 2.3 Create Buyers List

- Refine contact log with information for each potential buyer.
- Evaluate and screen buyers.
- Finalize buyer list.

**Output → Refined Buyers List:** Detailed list of financial sponsors and strategic buyers with fund sizes, assets, dry powder, M&A activity, and fit rationale.

### 2.4 Prepare Preliminary Virtual Data Room (VDR) for External Diligence

- Add core business materials to VDR (financials, product data) from initial sandbox.
- Organize VDR; build data narrative and gating plan for releasing information pre/post IOI and LOI.

**Output → Virtual Data Room:** Secure, consolidated repository for potential buyers to conduct due diligence on the client's business, including financials, supply chain, product performance, audits, contracts, etc.

### 2.5 Draft NDAs

- Legal drafts NDA.
- Bankers review or coordinate third-party review (e.g., Ontra).

**Output → NDA Template.**

### 2.6 Facilitate Third-Party Diligence

- Facilitate Quality of Earnings (required) by third party.
- Facilitate external market/brand study, if necessary.

### 2.7 Begin Building Integrated Financial Model

- Develop three-statement operating model.
- Review valuations with client and third-party advisors as necessary.

**Output → Integrated Financial Model:** Forward-looking model tied to the company's historical performance; forecasts future performance based on operating assumptions and drivers.

---

## Phase 3: Buyer Go-to-Market (~4 weeks)

**Objective:** Conduct marketing, send NDAs/CIMs/preliminary due diligence materials, and receive indications of interest from potential buyers.

### 3.1 Execute Buyer Outreach

- Launch outreach (phone calls, cold emails, targeted emails) and distribute NDAs/teasers.
- Distribute CIM to interested buyers.
- Draft bid letter (including diligence, financing, bid timeline, high-level offer terms).
- Maintain buyer contact log progress.

**Outputs:**
- **Buyer Contact Log:** Log of outreach, NDA progress, investor engagement, proposals, and pipeline status (logistics).
- **Signed NDAs.**

### 3.2 Draft Management Presentation Materials

- Work with client to structure main selling points; explain details of financial/strategic projections.
- Consolidate selling points and financials into management presentation.

**Output → Management Presentation:** Deep overview to potential buyers presenting the client's management team, business model, and growth narrative.

### 3.3 Operate 1st Round VDR Diligence

- Open up preliminary VDR.
- Buyers request information into company for due diligence.
- Conduct diligence calls and keep track of information requests from buyers.
- Monitor and review buyer due diligence request activity.

**Outputs:**
- **Buyer Diligence Tracker:** Buyer-specific diligence tracker customized for buyer requests across all diligence areas (financial, operational, legal, tax, insurance, HR).
- **Business Diligence Sessions:** Bank-to-buyer diligence meetings designed to give a deeper understanding of the client's business fundamentals and address any questions.

### 3.4 Receive Indications of Interest (IOI)

- Receive preliminary letters/indications of interest.
- Synthesize, analyze IOIs and prepare deck summary.

**Outputs:**
- **Indications of Intent:** Formal, non-binding letters from prospective buyers communicating initial interest in acquiring the client.
- **IOI Summary Deck:** Side-by-side comparison of received IOIs, to be reviewed with client.

---

## Phase 4: Detailed Buyer Diligence (~6 weeks)

**Objective:** Select second-round buyers for detailed diligence, coordinate diligence between buyers and client's management team, and receive letters of intent.

### 4.1 Shortlist Buyers for 2nd Round Diligence

- Work with client to select second-round buyers based on price and terms.
- Notify buyers selected and not selected; maintain relationship if appropriate.

### 4.2 Conduct Management Presentations

- Facilitate management presentations to introduce buyers to management team.
- Update buyers on new developments; address any concerns or questions.

### 4.3 Operate 2nd Round VDR Diligence

- Open up 2nd round diligence materials (audits, contracts, etc.).
- Distribute draft purchase and sale contract to second-round buyers.
- Monitor and coordinate due diligence.
- Conduct diligence calls and keep track of information requests from buyers.

**Outputs:**
- **Buyer Diligence Tracker:** Buyer-specific diligence tracker customized for buyer requests across all diligence areas (financial, operational, legal, tax, insurance, HR).
- **Business Diligence Sessions:** Buyer-facing diligence meetings designed to give a deeper understanding of the client's business fundamentals and address any questions.

### 4.4 Receive LOIs

- Issue final bid instructions letter (valuations, timelines, etc.) for LOIs.
- Receive LOIs with final bid offers.
- Prepare summary to evaluate terms of final bids.

**Outputs:**
- **Letter of Intent (LOI):** Buyer letter summarizing the broad terms of a potential final agreement, including purchase price.
- **LOI Summary Deck:** Presentation that stacks LOIs side-by-side to view, evaluate based on lenses (price, cash/non-cash considerations, etc.) and negotiate value.

---

## Phase 5: Receive Final Bids, Negotiate, and Complete Final Sale (~3 weeks)

**Objective:** Evaluate LOIs, negotiate with top buyer candidates, facilitate confirmatory diligence, and execute legal close.

### 5.1 Evaluate Bids and Negotiate

- Analyze buyer motivations/problems.
- Negotiate LOIs with top buyers.
- Coordinate legal, tax, and accounting considerations.

### 5.2 Finalize Legal Documentation

- Legal redlines purchase agreements and disclosure schedules.
- Bankers review for key business points that drive value or risk.
- Purchase agreements are amended as necessary.

**Outputs:**
- **Purchase Agreement:** Buyer purchase value and business points.
- **Disclosure Schedules:** All assets that are in-scope of the purchase.

### 5.3 Confirmatory Diligence

- Respond to final buyer diligence requests (e.g., tax, legal).

### 5.4 Finalize Sale

- If needed, run a Round 3 bid.
- Execute definitive agreement.
- Make announcement of sale.
